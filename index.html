The Hidden Dangers in Your Digital Routine

  • 88% of data breaches are caused by human error. – IBM
  • The average person reuses a password across 14+ accounts. – Google Survey
  • 25+ billion records have been breached globally. – HaveIBeenPwned

Reusing a password is like using the same key for your apartment and your diary—then dropping it in Times Square.

5-Minute Cyber Hygiene Checklist

  • Use a Password Manager – Try Bitwarden or 1Password.
  • Turn on Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) – Use Authy or Google Authenticator.
  • Revoke Excess App Permissions – Especially on Google and Facebook.
  • Avoid Public Wi-Fi – Or use a VPN like ProtonVPN.
  • Check Your Breach Status – Visit HaveIBeenPwned.

Cyberpsychology: Why We Fall for Scams

Scammers exploit urgency and fear. A fake email claiming "URGENT! Account Suspended" is enough to fool many of us.

What’s at Stake: The Bigger Picture

Cybercrime is expected to cost the world $10.5 trillion annually by 2025 – making it more profitable than the global drug trade. Your personal info—emails, passwords, browsing history—is currency for criminals.

Even your digital identity could be sold on the dark web for as little as $6. But cleaning up the mess after identity theft? That can cost months of recovery, lost opportunities, and emotional distress.
